    How Did Steve Bacic Pursue His Career? Professional Life

    Bacic made his acting debut in 1991 as Tommy Boylan Jr. in “21 Jump Street,” and the following year worked on “Street Justice,” in which he also had a small role. Throughout the early 1990s, he worked on projects like “Another Stakeout,” “Born to Run,” “The Commish,” and “M.A. N.T.I.S.” Steve then appeared as a guest star in three episodes of “The X-Files” before joining the cast of “Voyage of Terror” in 1998, where he worked alongside Lindsay Wagner, Michael Ironside, Martin Sheen, and Horst Buchholz.

    In the year 2000, Steve began playing Telemachus Rhade / Gaheris Rhade in “Andromeda,” a critically acclaimed T.V. series that received 16 awards, including a Certificate of Merit, a Gold Plaque, a Silver Plaque, and a Gemini. The following year, the actor played the role of a coach in “Just Deal,” and the following year, he joined the casts of several films and television series, including “Beyond Belief: Fact or Fiction,” “Ballistic: Ecks vs. Sever,” “Body & Soul,” and “Just Cause,” and then worked on “X-Men 2” until 2003.

    More On Career

    The actor had a lot on his plate in the mid-2000s, with projects like “John Tucker Must Die,” “Blade: The Series,” “Psych,” and “Whistler,” in which he played Adam Lawson. Bacic joined the cast of “Flash Gordon” the following year, and in 2008 he played Miro Da Silva in “The Guard.” In 2009, he began work on “Cra$h & Burn” as Pavel Korkov, and the following year, he portrayed Goran in “Big Love” until 2011.

    Steve’s most recent projects include his work on “Arrow,” a critically acclaimed series in which he played Sean Sonus.
    In 2018, he worked on several projects, including “Lemonade,” a film about a young Romanian woman who moves to the United States with her son and marries an American man she barely knows. He also worked on “Garage Sale Mystery: Pandora’s Box,” “Garage Sale Mystery: The Mask Murder,” “Garage Sale Mysteries: Picture a Murder,” and “Beyond the Call,” which is currently in post-production.
