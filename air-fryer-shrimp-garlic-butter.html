    For the Shrimp:

    • large shrimp – You’ll need about one pound, peeled, deveined, and with the tails on or off.
    • olive oil – This oil will add a touch of light flavor without taking away from the rest of the flavor profile.
    • kosher salt and black pepper – A touch to taste.
    • garlic powder and onion powder – To provide a base seasoning before coating in the sauce.

    For the Garlic Sauce:

    • butter – Unsalted is recommended.
    • garlic – Two cloves, minced.
    • lemon juice – Fresh squeezed is best.
    • garlic powder – For additional flavor.
    • kosher salt – Just a pinch.
    • red pepper flakes – These are optional but add a nice kick to the sauce.
    • parsley – Freshly chopped.

    Use a zester or garlic press to get finely minced garlic.

    How to Cook Shrimp in Air Fryer

    Air Fryer Shrimp dripping in garlic sauce couldn’t be easier! Family and friends will be requesting this dish weekly!

    For the Shrimp:

  • Preheat your air fryer to 400°F.
  • Place the shrimp in a large bowl or plastic ziploc bag. Next, add the olive oil, salt, garlic powder, onion powder, and black pepper. Use tongs (or your hands) to toss the shrimp in the bowl to fully coat the shrimp. Or simply seal the bag and shake to coat until all shrimp are evenly seasoned.
  • Before adding in the shrimp, spray the basket of the air fryer with nonstick cooking spray or olive oil spray. Next, place shrimp in air fryer in a single layer. Depending on how large the shrimp or how many you’ve decided to make, you may need to air fry the shrimp in batches.
  • Finally, air fry for 6 minutes or until the shrimp is cooked through. The shrimp should be pink, slightly curled, and opaque. Repeat with any remaining shrimp until all shrimp are cooked.

    • For the Garlic Sauce:

    While the shrimp are cooking, you’ll prepare the garlic butter sauce.

  • Start by melting the unsalted butter in a small saucepan over medium-low heat. Add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute, stirring continuously.
  • Next, remove the saucepan from the heat and whisk in the lemon juice, garlic powder, salt, red pepper flakes, and fresh parsley.
  • Finally, serve shrimp with the garlic sauce drizzled on top and enjoy!

    • FAQs for Air Fryer Shrimp

    How long will the Air Fryer Shrimp in Garlic Sauce stay fresh? This dish is best eaten right after it’s made. However, leftovers can be stored in an airtight container for up to 4 days in the fridge to retain the best flavor.

    Can frozen shrimp be used in the air fryer? Yes, you can use frozen shrimp in this recipe however, make sure they are deveined and do not have shells. You’ll need to add about 2 to 3 extra minutes of cook time to account for them being frozen. In addition, the seasoning also won’t stick to the frozen shrimp so you may want to sprinkle the salt and pepper and garlic and onion powder on the shrimp after they’ve cooked.

    Can the shrimp be coated in the garlic sauce prior to cooking? While you can go ahead and toss the shrimp in the garlic sauce before placing in the air fryer, it does make for a messier cleanup later. I recommend cooking the shrimp separately and drizzling with the garlic sauce afterwards to save yourself the trouble. The flavors will pair just as well as if they had been cooked together.

    Tips and Variations

    • Depending on how large your shrimp are, you may need to adjust the cooking time in your air fryer. 
    • Coat your shrimp in panko before cooking for more of a fried shrimp texture.
    • Mixing honey into the garlic sauce gives a sweet flavor that pairs well with the savory and spicy if using the red pepper flakes.
    • A quick squeeze of fresh lemon juice over the shrimp before serving for an extra burst of flavor. 
    • A touch of Parmesan is a great garnish as well!

    Air Fryer Shrimp

    Easy Air Fryer Shrimp is topped with a delectable garlic herb butter sauce and ready in just minutes! Shrimp cooked to perfection in the air fryer and drizzled with a buttery, savory, garlic sauce is just the thing for an amazing appetizer or to top off an incredible meal. Don’t forget the splash of fresh lemon juice!

    Course Appetizer, Dinner, Entree, Main Course, Main Dish Cuisine American Keyword air fryer shrimp, air fryer shrimp recipes, shrimp in air fryer Prep Time 5 minutes minutes Cook Time 10 minutes minutes Servings 4 Calories 322kcal Author Trish – Mom On Timeout

    Ingredients

    • 1 pound large shrimp peeled, deveined, tails on or off (26- 30 count)
    • 2 teaspoons olive oil
    • ½ teaspoon kosher salt
    • ½ teaspoon black pepper
    • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
    • ½ teaspoon onion powder

    For the garlic sauce:

    • 8 tablespoons unsalted butter
    • 2 cloves garlic minced
    • 2 teaspoons lemon juice fresh squeezed
    • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
    • ½ teaspoon kosher salt
    • ¼ teaspoon red pepper flakes optional
    • 1 tablespoon fresh chopped parsley

    Instructions

    • Preheat the air fryer to 400°F.

    • Place the shrimp in a large bowl or plastic ziploc bag. Pour in the olive oil, salt, pepper, garlic powder and onion powder. Use tongs to toss the shrimp in the bowl to fully coat the shrimp or simply seal the bag and shake to coat.

      1 pound large shrimp, 2 teaspoons olive oil, ½ teaspoon kosher salt, ½ teaspoon garlic powder, ½ teaspoon onion powder, ½ teaspoon black pepper

    • Spray the basket of the air fryer with nonstick cooking spray or olive oil spray. Place the shrimp into the air fryer in a single layer (You may need to air fry the shrimp in batches).

    • Air fry for 6 minutes or until the shrimp is cooked through. The shrimp should be pink, slightly curled, and opaque. Repeat with the remaining shrimp until all shrimp are cooked.

    Garlic Sauce

    • While the shrimp are cooking, melt the butter in a small saucepan over medium-low heat. Add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute, stirring continuously.

      8 tablespoons unsalted butter, 2 cloves garlic

    • Remove the saucepan from the heat and whisk in lemon juice, garlic powder, salt, red pepper flakes (if using), and parsley.

      2 teaspoons lemon juice, ½ teaspoon garlic powder, ½ teaspoon kosher salt, ¼ teaspoon red pepper flakes, 1 tablespoon fresh chopped parsley

    • Serve shrimp with garlic sauce drizzled on top.
